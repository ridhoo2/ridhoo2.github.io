Yayasan PLAN

Kojic acid is a fungal metabolic product produced by a few species of Aspergillus, especially by A. oryzae, which has the Japanese common name koji. This acid is a by-product in the fermentation process of malting rice, for use in the manufacturing of sake, the Japanese rice wine. Kojic acid was first isolated in 1907 by Saito from mycelia of A. oryzae grown on steamed rice. In 1912 Yabuta gave it the name kojic acid, and only in 1924 he deciphered the correct structure of the molecule of this acid.
